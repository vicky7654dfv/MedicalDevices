A PPM contract covers scheduled inspections, calibration, lubrication, part replacements, electrical safety testing (IEC 60601), and full compliance documentation. Frequency is agreed upfront — typically quarterly or bi-annually — and can be customised based on manufacturer recommendations and usage intensity.

Standard MRI calibration runs 4–8 hours if the system is accessible and the phantom is provided. CT scanner calibration, including detector offset and water phantom testing, typically requires 2–4 hours. We schedule all imaging work during non-peak hours to minimise disruption to clinical workflow.

Our engineers are OEM-agnostic. We service equipment from Siemens, Philips, GE Healthcare, Mindray, Dragerwerk, Karl Storz, and over 60 other manufacturers. For specialised platforms we maintain direct service manuals and OEM-authorised parts channels to ensure full compatibility and warranty protection.

All services are performed in compliance with ISO 13485:2016, IEC 60601-1, AAMI/ANSI standards, FDA 21 CFR Part 820, CE Marking directives, and WHO Medical Device Regulations. Country-specific regulatory requirements are incorporated as part of every engagement.

Under our critical-care SLA, our response time is under 4 hours for on-site attendance, 24/7/365. Remote diagnostic support can typically begin within 30 minutes of a ticket being raised. All emergency calls are triaged by a senior biomedical engineer to ensure the fastest possible restoration of critical equipment.

All our biomedical engineers are full-time MediLife employees who undergo rigorous internal training programmes, OEM certification, and annual competency assessments. We do not subcontract any technical work, ensuring consistent quality, accountability, and continuity of relationship for every client.
